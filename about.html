My Story

Hi, I’m Mommo — a travel photographer and storyteller drawn to the mountains, lakes, and wildlife of East Africa.

My journey began with simple walks and borrowed cameras, chasing light across hillsides and watching how the world softened at sunrise and sharpened at dusk. Over time, those quiet moments turned into a calling: to document the places and creatures that move me, and to share the stories that live between them.

From misty mornings in the Volcanoes, to long drives across the savannah, to still evenings by Lake Kivu, I’ve learned that travel is less about ticking off destinations and more about paying attention — to the land, to the people, and to the silence in between.

Mommo Travels is my way of inviting you into that way of seeing: slower, softer, and always a little bit in awe.

How I See Travel & Photography

Every journey is a conversation between place and person. My work sits at the intersection of observation, respect, and story — guided by a deep love for East Africa’s wild spaces and the lives that move through them.

Slow Travel

I prefer to linger — to return to the same trail, the same shoreline, the same village — and notice what changes, and what doesn’t.

Respect for Wildlife

The animals I photograph are not props; they are beings with their own rhythms and boundaries. My work is always at a respectful distance.

Story Over Spectacle

I’m less interested in the perfect postcard and more in the small, honest details that make a place feel alive and real.
