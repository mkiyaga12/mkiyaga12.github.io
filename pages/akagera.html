Akagera National Park

Established in 1934, Akagera is Rwanda’s largest national park and is known for its incredible biodiversity. It’s home to the “Big Five” (lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhino) as well as other wildlife like giraffes, zebras, hippos, and various antelope species. Birdwatchers will also find Akagera particularly exciting, with over 480 bird species recorded.
